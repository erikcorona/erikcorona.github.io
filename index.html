About

I'm a data scientist who likes building things that work. For the past 16 years I've been turning messy, real world data into models and pipelines that actually ship. Most of my work has been in healthcare, where the data is complicated and the stakes are high.

I got my Ph.D. in Biomedical Informatics at Stanford and did a postdoc at Harvard, where I published in PLoS Genetics, PNAS, and RECOMB. Since then I've been in industry, building and leading data science teams, designing ML infrastructure, and trying to make sure the things we build are useful and not just clever.

These days I spend a lot of time on embeddings, deep learning for cost prediction, and figuring out how to get AI tools into the hands of people who need them. I also speak Spanish.
